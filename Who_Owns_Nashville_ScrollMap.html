Before the wave

By 2000, fewer than 2,000 Davidson County single-family homes had left owner-occupant hands.

The foreclosure era

Through 2008 the count reaches about 4,300. Acquisitions stay modest.

Investors arrive in volume

By 2013 the total passes 6,500 and the yearly pace climbs sharply.

The surge

2017 alone adds more than 1,000 homes. The running total tops 10,000.

Peak acquisition

2021 is the single biggest year, adding over 2,100 homes.

Where we are now

The cumulative total reaches 22,627 single-family homes held by non-occupant owners.
Source: Davidson County Assessor / Register of Deeds parcel timeline. Each dot is one home, placed in the year it left owner-occupancy.
