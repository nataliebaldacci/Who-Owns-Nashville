Methodology. Each dot is a single-family home (federal def, H. Res. 1299 § 1001(3): DUCount 1–2, no mobile/multistory/non-residential) that is non-owner-occupied in the current cadastral (owner mailing zip ≠ property zip), placed in the year its current owner acquired it via an arm's-length deed (sale price > $1,000, bulk deeds price-allocated). 22,627 of 42,446 current non-owner-occupied homes have such a deed (53%); the rest were acquired by quitclaim, inheritance, or foreclosure, or lack a recorded price. Sources: ParcelHistory ownership (Layer 2) + Cadastral (Layer 4). Davidson County.
