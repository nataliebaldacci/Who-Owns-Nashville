Rental Housing Surplus / Deficit, by Census Tract

Vacancy-rate method · equilibrium = 2012–2018 county average · current = 2020–2024 ACS · Davidson County, Tennessee

How this is calculated

For each tract, the renter housing deficit is the number of rental units it is short of a balanced market, as a share of its housing stock:

deficit = (equilibrium vacancy − current vacancy) × rental stock

Equilibrium = Davidson County's average vacancy over 2012–2018 (rental 6.44%, owner 2.11%). Current = 2020–2024 ACS. Tracts ≥5% short are "substantially undersupplied," ±2% is "close to balance," ≥5% surplus is "oversupplied." Method follows Moody's Analytics, Reinvestment Fund & PolicyMap, Bringing the Housing Shortage Into Sharper Focus (July 2025).
