Who Owns Nashville?

Interactive maps of institutional and corporate ownership of residential housing in Davidson County, Tennessee. Built entirely from primary government data — every parcel traces to a live county record.

Owner Portfolio Explorer

Search any owner. The map highlights every home in that owner's portfolio — all the LLCs and shells linked to the same entity by name and mailing address.

Corporate Rental Growth, 2008–2026

A decade-plus of corporate-owned single-family rental registrations. Toggle corporate-only, structure type (detached vs. attached), and watch cumulative vs. still-active.

Active Rentals — Property Detail

Every currently-active corporate single-family rental, clickable. Each dot opens the bed/bath, square footage, year built, purchase year, registration history, and direct county links (parcel viewer, property card, aerial, deed, permit).

Corporate Acquisition Timeline

When corporate owners bought their homes, year by year — the build-up of concentration across the last decade.

Corporate-Owned Parcels

Every corporate-owned single-family parcel as its actual lot shape, colored by parent operator.

Concentration Hexmap

Where corporate ownership clusters across the county, binned into hexagons.

Build-to-Rent: Current vs. Pipeline

Built-and-held rental homes versus vacant lots still in the development pipeline, by operator.

When Nashville's Single-Family Stock Was Built

Every single-family home (federal definition), placed by year built. Play the slider to watch the county grow; filter by detached, attached, or duplex.

Current Single-Family Property Types

The whole single-family stock colored by structure type, with an owner-occupied vs. non-owner filter and live stats.

When Homes Became Non-Owner-Occupied

Every home that is absentee-owned today, placed in the year its current owner acquired it. 83% changed hands since 2010. (Arm's-length companion also available.)

Single-Family Homes Never Owner-Occupied

The 8,977 single-family parcels where no owner in the entire chain of title ever lived in the home — pure investor stock, skewed to condos and townhomes.

What Happened to Foreclosed Homes

Every parcel sold by Trustee's Deed, 2001–today. Red at foreclosure, then green or orange as it returns to a resident or an investor, grey when the parcel is retired. Filter by status today.

How Land Banking Unfolds

Starts with the vacant land in 2000 and plays forward — watch each parcel get acquired by a land banker, subdivided, then built into homes. The acquire → subdivide → build sequence, year by year.

Land-Bank Footprints

The actual shape of the land each investor controls, rebuilt from every parcel it ever owned — Walton, Drapac, Green Trails, Domain Timberlake and more, by acreage.

Who Land-Banks Nashville?

Large vacant residential tracts held by out-of-state and investor entities. Each tract shows held-years, the buyer, and what it became — still banking, or built into homes.

Holdings vs. Landlord Registration

Every operator's corporate homes vs. their active landlord registrations. Red dots are unregistered. Of 4,895 corporate homes, only 53% are registered.

Reconstructed Retired Parcels

Parcels that no longer exist, rebuilt by merging the lots they were subdivided into — the original tract boundary before condo conversion or re-platting.

Institutional Ownership by Census Tract

Choropleth of the share of single-family homes owned by corporate / institutional entities, by census tract.

Institutional Single-Family Homes (Dot Map)

Every single-family home owned by a corporate / institutional entity as one dot — the GAO-style density view. 34,274 homes, concentrated southeast.

Big Operators vs. All Single-Family Homes

Grey = every single-family home in Davidson County; colored dots = homes held by the major institutional operators (Progress, AMH, Amherst, Tricon…). Where the big players actually sit.
